Frequently Asked Questions

What's included in The DTV Guide?
The guide is a comprehensive PDF covering every step of the DTV application: eligibility requirements, document preparation, embassy-specific nuances, common rejection reasons, and my AI-assisted formatting system. You also get templates, checklists, and 12 months of updates.
How is this different from free info online?
Free info is often outdated, generic, or wrong. Embassy requirements change. The DTV Guide is kept current, includes embassy-specific details, and gives you the exact document formatting system I built — which isn't documented anywhere else. Plus, the £300 government fee is non-refundable. Getting it wrong is expensive.
Can I apply from inside Thailand?
In most cases, no. The DTV is designed to be applied for from your home country or a country where you have residency. If you're already in Thailand on a tourist visa, you'll typically need to leave to apply. The guide covers the best strategies for your situation.
What if I don't have £11,500 saved yet?
The 500,000 THB requirement is strict — you need to show it in your account. If you don't have it yet, the consultation call can help you plan your timeline. I won't sell you the guide if you're not ready.
Is this guaranteed to get me approved?
No — embassies make the final decision. What I guarantee is that you'll submit the strongest possible application with correctly formatted documents. I've used this exact system myself and helped others get approved. I also guarantee you'll save 10+ hours of research.
How long does the DTV last?
The DTV is a 5-year multiple entry visa. Each stay can be up to 180 days. You can extend once per entry for another 180 days (for a fee), meaning you could potentially stay continuously for up to 1 year before needing a border run.
