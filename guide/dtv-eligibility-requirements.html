DTV Visa Eligibility Requirements

The complete checklist: age, citizenship, proof of funds, passport validity, and remote work documentation.

To qualify for Thailand's DTV visa, you must be at least 20 years old, hold a passport from an eligible country, have at least 500,000 THB in a bank account, and be able to work remotely for employers or clients outside Thailand.

Complete Eligibility Checklist

Age Requirement

You must be at least 20 years old at the time of application. There is no upper age limit.

Citizenship

You must be a citizen of an approved country. Eligibility depends on citizenship, not country of residence. The DTV is available to citizens of the UK, Ireland, the US, Canada, Australia, EU member states, and many other approved countries.

Proof of Funds

Bank balance of at least 500,000 THB (approximately $14,000 USD / £11,000 GBP). Funds must be in a bank account, not investments or cryptocurrency. Learn more about proof of funds →

Remote Work Status

You must be able to demonstrate that you work remotely for employers, clients, or platforms located outside Thailand. This applies to employed remote workers, freelancers, self-employed individuals, and business owners. Learn about remote work documentation →

Passport Validity

Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay in Thailand and have at least one blank page for the visa stamp.

Health Insurance (Optional)

While not always strictly required for the initial visa application, Thai immigration may request proof of health insurance covering your stay. Many applicants obtain insurance as a precaution.

Eligible Countries

The DTV visa is available to citizens of a defined list of countries. This list is subject to change, so always verify current eligibility before applying. Generally includes:

Major Eligible Countries

  • North America: United States, Canada
  • Europe: UK, Ireland, all EU member states, Norway, Switzerland
  • Oceania: Australia, New Zealand
  • Asia: Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Hong Kong (varies)

Not a complete list. Use our eligibility quiz for the most up-to-date country list.

Remote Work Categories

The DTV visa accommodates different types of remote workers:

  • Remote Employees: Working for a company outside Thailand
  • Self-Employed/Freelancers: Working for clients outside Thailand
  • Digital Nomads: Content creators, influencers, online business owners
  • Company Directors: Running a business registered outside Thailand

Each category has slightly different documentation requirements. See the self-employed guide →

What Disqualifies You?

These factors will likely result in visa rejection:

  • Under 20 years of age
  • Citizenship from a non-eligible country
  • Insufficient proof of funds (below 500,000 THB)
  • Unable to demonstrate remote work capability
  • Passport expiring within 6 months
  • Criminal record or immigration violations in Thailand
