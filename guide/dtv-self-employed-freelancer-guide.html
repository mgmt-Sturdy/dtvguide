Self-Employed & Freelancer DTV Guide

The specific documentation path for self-employed applicants: tax registration, invoices, contracts, and income evidence.

Self-employed applicants and freelancers are eligible for the DTV visa provided they can document remote income from clients or platforms located outside Thailand, along with proof of funds and, where applicable, tax registration or filed tax returns.

Required Documentation

Tax Registration or Tax Returns

Filed tax returns showing your self-employment income are highly preferred. If you don't file taxes (common in some countries), provide evidence of tax registration or business registration.

Client Contracts or Invoices

Provide contracts, invoices, or engagement letters from the past 6-12 months showing ongoing work with clients outside Thailand.

Platform Earnings (if applicable)

For Upwork, Fiverr, or other platforms, provide statements showing your earnings and client locations.

Business Registration (optional)

If you have a registered business, include your registration certificate. Not required for sole traders in many cases.

Proof of Funds

Bank balance of 500,000 THB minimum, held for at least 2-3 months. Learn more →

Remote Work Statement for Self-Employed

Your statement should explain:

  • Your profession/service offering
  • How you find and work with clients remotely
  • Confirmation all clients are outside Thailand
  • Duration of your self-employment/freelance work
  • Your ability to continue working from Thailand

Sample Statement Opening

"I am a freelance [profession] working remotely for clients located in [countries]. I have been operating as a freelancer for [duration], and my work is conducted entirely online, requiring only an internet connection. All my clients and income sources are located outside Thailand..."

Common Self-Employed Scenarios

Digital Marketers & Consultants

Provide: Client contracts, invoices for recent projects, LinkedIn profile or professional website, tax returns.

Content Creators & Influencers

Provide: Platform analytics, sponsorship contracts, ad revenue statements, tax documents for content income.

Developers & Designers

Provide: Portfolio website, client contracts, GitHub/GitLab profile, platform earnings (if using freelance sites).

E-commerce & Online Business Owners

Provide: Business registration, sales records, platform statements (Shopify, Amazon), tax documents.

What If You Don't Have Tax Documents?

Not all countries require freelancers to register or file taxes. If this applies to you:

  • Provide extensive client documentation (contracts + invoices)
  • Include a statement explaining your country's tax requirements
  • Provide bank statements showing consistent business income
  • Consider business registration if available in your jurisdiction

Strong vs. Weak Applications

Strong Application

  • 2+ years of consistent freelance income
  • Multiple long-term clients with contracts
  • Filed tax returns showing self-employment
  • Professional website/portfolio
  • Platform earnings statements (if applicable)

Weak Application

  • Started freelancing last month
  • No contracts or formal agreements
  • Inconsistent income
  • No tax documents or registration
  • Single client (looks like disguised employment)
