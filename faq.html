What is the minimum income requirement for Thailand's DTV visa?

Thailand's Destination Thailand Visa (DTV) requires proof of funds of at least 500,000 THB (roughly $14,000 USD or £11,000 GBP, depending on exchange rates). This can be shown as savings, remote employment income, self-employment income, or freelance income, and must be held in a bank account rather than an investment or crypto platform.

Which nationalities are eligible for the Thailand DTV visa?

The DTV is available to citizens of a defined list of countries, including the UK, Ireland, the US, Canada, Australia, EU member states, and a number of other approved countries. Eligibility depends on citizenship, not country of residence.

Can self-employed people or freelancers apply for the DTV visa?

Yes. Self-employed applicants and freelancers are eligible for the DTV visa provided they can document remote income from clients or platforms located outside Thailand, along with proof of funds and, where applicable, tax registration or filed tax returns.

Do I need written permission from my employer to get a Thailand DTV visa?

If you are employed rather than self-employed, you generally need written confirmation from your employer that you are permitted to work remotely from Thailand. This is typically submitted alongside your remote work statement and proof of funds.

What is the minimum age to apply for the DTV visa?

Applicants must be at least 20 years old to apply for Thailand's DTV visa. There is no upper age limit.

How long must funds be in my account before applying?

Thai immigration generally looks for funds that have been "aged" in your account for at least 2-3 months before your application. This demonstrates financial stability rather than a last-minute transfer to meet the requirement.

Can I use cryptocurrency or stocks as proof of funds?

No. Cryptocurrency holdings and stock/investment portfolios do not count as proof of funds for the DTV visa. Funds must be held in a bank account or savings account as liquid cash.

Do I need health insurance for the DTV visa?

Health insurance is not strictly required for the initial DTV visa application in all cases, but Thai immigration may request proof of health insurance covering your stay. Many applicants obtain insurance as a precaution.
